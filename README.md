# HIAA-Strategic-Growth-Analysis
Strategic growth analysis for Halifax International Airport Authority(HIAA): Evaluating investment tradeoffs between seasonal European tourism and year-round flights to the US.

## Executive Summary

In 2025, Halifax Stanfield (YHZ) achieved a milestone by serving a record **4.1 million passengers**, marking a significant new growth phase for Atlantic Canada’s primary gateway (HIAA, 2026). This growth was fueled by two distinct trends: a **19.3% surge** in international travelers, driven largely by WestJet’s seasonal transatlantic expansion, and an **8.5% increase** in transborder (U.S.) traffic, supported by Porter Airlines’ new year-round jet service (HIAA, 2026; Tourism Nova Scotia, 2025).

The airport’s Senior Leadership Team now faces a critical choice regarding the allocation of limited air service incentive funding for the 2026-2027 fiscal year. While high-capacity summer flights to Europe generate massive seasonal revenue—with air travelers spending an average of **$1,224 per person**—they often result in underutilized airport infrastructure during winter months (Tourism Nova Scotia, 2024). Conversely, year-round transborder routes through airlines like Porter offer economic stability and consistent local employment but typically provide lower immediate margins per passenger (HIAA, 2025).

This project analyzes which investment strategy better supports Nova Scotia’s long-term economic resilience. By evaluating the trade-offs between seasonal "value" and year-round "volume," this analysis provides a data-driven recommendation to help HIAA leadership maximize the airport’s regional economic impact.
